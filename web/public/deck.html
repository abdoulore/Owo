An amount, a note, a name.

The sender signs in with Google, types what they're sending and who it's from, and gets a link.

Sharing the link is sending the money. Any chat app works. Nothing to install on the other side.

One tap and it's theirs.

The recipient opens the link, signs in with their own Google, and taps Claim.

Confetti. The money is in their balance, and the sender's activity now reads To Ade · Claimed.

No wallet. No seed phrase.
No gas. No chain.

Nobody in that story touched crypto. Yet every payment settled on Arbitrum, and the receipt is one tap away on Arbiscan for anyone who wants proof.

What's actually underneath.

Google sign-inMagic silently provisions a signing key. No extension, no app.
→
Smart accountThe key owns a ZeroDev Kernel account: the user's on-chain identity.
→
EscrowRemitEscrow locks USDC on Arbitrum against the hash of the link's secret.
→
RelayerSubmits claims and pays gas, alongside a sponsored paymaster for user calls.

"Continue with Google" is secretly the entire wallet ceremony. A sponsored paymaster covers every drop of gas.

A link that can't be stolen.

A check made out to cash

Funds lock against the hash of a secret that exists only in the link's URL fragment. It is never stored on any server.

A real vulnerability, found and closed

A reverted claim would expose the secret in calldata. Now claim() reverts for anyone but the relayer on-chain, and the API locks each link to its first claimant, so a leaked secret cannot be redirected.

Proven, not promised

15 Foundry tests including a fuzz test, all green. Contracts deployed and verified on Arbiscan.
